ASSESSING BUILDING DAMAGE

IMPACT OF NATURAL DISASTERS BASED ON SATELLITE IMAGES

We obtained the data from xview2 website for six different types of natural disasters around the world, covering a total area of over 45,361 square kilometers. Our dataset of 850,736 buildings has building polygons, classification labels for damage types, ordinal labels of damage level, and corresponding satellite metadata. The xBD dataset has 15 countries and 6 types of disasters.

One of the most devastating natural disaster that is a threat to human life and destroy millions of properties. In our project we analyze the earthquake data of Mexico
Earthquakes can easily rupture natural gas mains and water mains, both contributing to the ignition of fires and hindering the efforts to control them. In our project we analyze the wildfire data of Portugal, socal-fire, pinery-bushfire, woolsey and Santa Rosa.
Flood is one of the deadliest types of natural disaster which results from extreme rain, snow, storm surges and coastal storms. In our project we analyze the flood data of midwest and nepal.

A Hurricane is a large rotating storm with high velocity winds that forms over warm waters in tropical areas. The shear strength of hurricanes winds can destroy buildings, topple trees and damage infrastructure. In our project we analyze the hurricane data of Matthew, Michael, Harvey and Florence.
